• Carwash Classifier

About

We are interested in the flow of traffic at a carwash near our house. To measure this, we photograph the queue at set intervals and associate with each image a label, the number of cars in line, and a timestamp. We then train a classifier to predict the number of cars in line given the image as input. Ultimately, we will extract time series to determine if there are any patterns in usage. For instance, do more people wash their car on the weekend? Or, do we see an increase in the number of carwashes following rain? In addition, we'll periodically update this page with statistics about the performance of our model.

The key phrase in the above paragraph is "sufficient confidence". We need as much training data as possible, but before we try to apply more advanced techniques such as autotagging, we have to manually label our images. If you'd like to contribute to our little project, please tag away!

How many cars are in line for the carwash:

Keep in mind that our view is obstructed. When one car is visible beyond the fence, there are often 1-2 cars ahead in line but out of sight. A good rule of thumb is # of visible cars + U, where U~Uniform({1, 2}).
